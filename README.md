# Movie-Visualization-Analysis
Business Challenge:
* Netflix would like you to complete an analysis of Movies from 2012 to 2016, to identify the most effective strategy for allocating investment toward adding a new catalog of older movies on their streaming platform. "
Key Insights:
Dataset:
* Box Office Performance Data
2012-2016
Fields: Movie titles, release year, director, leading actors), budget, revenue
1. ROI is concentrated in low-budget films
• Films under ~$20M achieve ~6x average ROI vs ~2x for mid/high budgets
• Top performers reach 70-100x ROI, almost exclusively in low-budget tiers
• High-budget films deliver more consistent but limited returns
2. Genre performance diverges across metrics
• Horror leads ROI (*500+), significantly outperforming all genres
• Action dominates revenue ("$25B+) but delivers only moderate efficiency
• Revenue = value - high demand does not guarantee strong returns
3. Breakout success follows a clear pattern
• Majority of high-ROI films fall under ™$50M budgets
• ~70% of top ROI films are Horror
• Increasing production volume shows diminishing returns
Strategic Recommendation
Adopt a dual investment strategy that maximizes ROI while sustaining audience demand
1. Prioritize high-ROI, low-budget films (Core Strategy)
2. Selectively acquire high-demand, high-revenue
• Focus on films under "$40M with high ROl potential
titles (Selective Strategy)
(>20x)
• Target high-efficiency genres, especially Horror
• Allocate a portion of budget to proven audience drivers
• Capture asymmetric upside with minimal capital
• Focus on Action, Adventure, and franchise-based films
Examples:
• The Devil Inside, Unfriended, Annabelle (70-100xROI)
